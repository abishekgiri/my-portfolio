About

Backend engineering with a systems lens and a bias toward reliability.

This page fills in the context behind the projects: how I think about systems, what I’m trying to learn, and the work that shaped how I operate.

I’m a Computer Science student building backend systems, distributed infrastructure, and research-oriented software that has to make sense under load, under failure, and under review.

A lot of my work is shaped by the same instincts: make state transitions explicit, make behavior testable, and make failure easier to reason about than success-path demos usually allow.

That has led me toward release governance, Raft-based storage, financial ledgers, schedulers, AI robustness research, and open-source contributions across larger public codebases.
